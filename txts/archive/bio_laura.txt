Laura Kurgan is a Professor of Architecture at the Graduate School of Architecture Planning and Preservation at Columbia University, where she directs the Masters of Science in Computational Design Practices, and the Center for Spatial Research(CSR). She is the author of Close Up at a Distance: Mapping, Technology, and Politics (Zone Books, 2013) and Co-Editor of Ways of Knowing Cities (Columbia Books on Architecture, 2019). She is working on a book on Conflict Urbanism presenting and reflecting upon ten years of collaborative work at CSR, forthcoming from Zone Books in 2026.
